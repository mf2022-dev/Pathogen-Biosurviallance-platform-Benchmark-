Key Findings

Critical insights from evaluating 169 platforms across 10 dimensions

Critical Gap

Genomic Sequencing Inequity

80% of sequencing capacity concentrated in Five Eyes + EU nations. Africa, Central Asia, and Pacific Islands have near-zero autonomous capability, creating massive surveillance blind spots.

Security Strongest

Security Leads All Dimensions

Security & Compliance scores highest at 82.5 average across all platforms. Defense-tier (L3) platforms show particularly strong security postures, but accessibility (73.9 avg) remains the weakest dimension.

Top Performer

Nextstrain Leads at 95/100

Only 3 platforms achieve Excellent tier (90+): Nextstrain, SORMAS, and outbreak.info. These represent the gold standard in phylogenetic analysis, outbreak management, and data aggregation respectively.

Geopolitical

Adversary Capabilities Growing

Russia (VECTOR), China (AMMS), DPRK, and Iran all maintain active biological research programs with dual-use potential. China's AI-integrated biosurveillance advancing rapidly.

AI/ML Divide

AI Adoption Highly Uneven

Only ~15% of platforms integrate meaningful AI/ML capabilities. DARPA-funded programs (PREEMPT, P3) lead in AI-driven biosurveillance, while LMIC platforms largely lack any AI integration.

Interoperability

Data Silos Persist

Interoperability averages 77.3/100. HL7/FHIR adoption remains inconsistent. Most defense platforms operate in classified silos, limiting Five Eyes data fusion potential despite treaty obligations.

Methodology

Pathogen Surveillance Evaluation Framework (PSEF) v3.0.0

Evaluation Process

Platform Identification

Systematic scan of government, academic, open-source, and defense biosurveillance platforms worldwide. 169 unique platforms identified across 164 functional categories.

Multi-Dimensional Scoring

Each platform scored 0-100 across 10 weighted dimensions using standardized rubrics aligned with WHO, CDC, ISO 27799:2025, and FDA AI/ML guidance.

Profile Enrichment

All 169 platforms enriched with 7-field profiles (overview, functional scope, tech stack, operator, data model, users/scale, access model) from verified sources.

Layer Classification

Five capability layers: L1 Surveillance (63), L2 Genomic (57), L3 Defense (35), L4 Hardware (9), L5 Policy (5) — reflecting the bio-intelligence stack.

Regional & Adversary Analysis

Platforms mapped to 8 geopolitical regions. Adversary capabilities assessed for Russia, China, DPRK, and Iran using open-source intelligence and published assessments.
