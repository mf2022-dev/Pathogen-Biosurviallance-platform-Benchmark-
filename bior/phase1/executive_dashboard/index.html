30-Second Brief

Global biodefense readiness is uneven. Of 169 evaluated platforms, only 3 (1.8%) achieve excellence (≥90), while 15 (8.9%) remain in developing status (<70). The Five Eyes alliance leads in capability breadth, but critical interoperability gaps persist — particularly at the classified-to-unclassified bridge and One Health integration points.

Key finding: Allied nations possess 74% of high-performing platforms (Good+Excellent tier), but adversary states (Russia, China) are rapidly closing genomic and AI-driven surveillance gaps. North Korea and Iran remain largely opaque (NATO Tier 5).

Priority action: Invest in cross-border data sharing infrastructure and classification-bridging protocols. The 3-10 year window is critical — current capability advantages will erode without coordinated alliance investment.
